Show that if $A \in \mathcal{L}(V)$ satisfying $A^2 -A+I=0$, then $A$ is invertible.
\begin{mybox}
\begin{proof}
\begin{align*}
    A^2 -A+I=0
    \iff \;& I = A - A^2\\
    \iff \;& I = A(I-A)\\
    \iff \;& I = (I-A)A
\end{align*}
Therefore $A$ is invertible and $A^{-1} = I-A$.
\end{proof}
\end{mybox}
